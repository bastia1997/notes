# A–Z Glossary

This glossary collects every term you'll meet across the site. Each entry lists the English term followed by the Italian translation in parentheses `(IT: ...)`, a 1–3 sentence definition, and a pointer to the relevant section. Where a term was born in English (most financial acronyms) the Italian form is identical: we keep the acronym for clarity.

## A

- **Alpha (IT: alfa)** — Portfolio return in excess of what CAPM predicts. Measures manager skill net of beta exposure. (→ sec. 20)
- **AML, Anti-Money Laundering (IT: antiriciclaggio)** — Set of regulations forcing banks and intermediaries to verify the origin of funds and report suspicious activity. (→ sec. 32)
- **AMM, Automated Market Maker (IT: AMM)** — DeFi protocol that prices swaps automatically via a mathematical formula (e.g. $x \cdot y = k$) instead of an order book. (→ sec. 41)
- **Annuity (IT: annualità, rendita)** — Series of periodic equal payments over a fixed horizon. Mortgage payments under the French schedule are an annuity. (→ sec. 11)
- **APP, Asset Purchase Programme (IT: APP)** — Programme through which a central bank buys securities on the secondary market to inject liquidity. Technical name for QE in the euro area. (→ sec. 3)
- **Arbitrage (IT: arbitraggio)** — Risk-free trade that exploits price discrepancies for the same asset across markets. In theory arbitrage windows close fast. (→ sec. 22)
- **Asset allocation (IT: asset allocation)** — Decision on how to split a portfolio across broad asset classes (equity, fixed income, cash, alternatives). Drives most of the return variance. (→ sec. 18)
- **AT1, Additional Tier 1 (IT: AT1)** — Subordinated bank capital instrument that absorbs losses via conversion or write-down. Riskier than CET1, less than senior debt. (→ sec. 30)
- **At-the-money, ATM (IT: ATM)** — Option whose strike equals the underlying spot price. Highest sensitivity to volatility (vega). (→ sec. 23)
- **Auction (IT: asta)** — Mechanism for placing government bonds (e.g. competitive auction for BTPs, Dutch auction for some Treasuries). (→ sec. 13)

## B

- **Bail-in (IT: bail-in)** — Bank resolution method where losses are absorbed by shareholders and creditors (in seniority order) instead of taxpayers. Introduced in the EU by the BRRD. (→ sec. 30)
- **Bank of Italy (IT: Banca d'Italia)** — Italian national central bank, part of the Eurosystem. Supervises Italian banks and runs the payment system. (→ sec. 32)
- **Basis point, bp (IT: punto base)** — One hundredth of a percentage point (0.01%). A move from 4.00% to 4.25% is "+25 bp". (→ sec. 12)
- **Benchmark (IT: benchmark)** — Reference index used to evaluate a portfolio or fund. E.g. MSCI World for global equities. (→ sec. 18)
- **Beta (IT: beta)** — Sensitivity of a security's return to the market. Beta = 1 → moves with the market; beta = 1.5 amplifies moves by 50%. (→ sec. 19)
- **BIC, Bank Identifier Code (IT: BIC)** — International code that identifies a bank for wire transfers. Synonym for SWIFT code, 8 or 11 chars. (→ sec. 7)
- **BIS, Bank for International Settlements (IT: BIS)** — Bank for International Settlements, based in Basel. Forum where the Basel accords are negotiated. (→ sec. 30)
- **Black-Scholes (IT: Black-Scholes)** — Closed-form formula for European option pricing under geometric Brownian motion. Earned Scholes and Merton the 1997 Nobel Prize. (→ sec. 23)
- **Blockchain (IT: blockchain)** — Distributed ledger of transactions organized into cryptographically linked blocks. Technological base of Bitcoin and other cryptos. (→ sec. 41)
- **BNPL, Buy Now Pay Later (IT: BNPL)** — Deferred-payment service offered at e-commerce checkout, typically interest-free. Under growing scrutiny because it may foster over-indebtedness. (→ sec. 40)
- **Bond (IT: obbligazione)** — Debt security. Pays a coupon and returns face value at maturity. (→ sec. 12)
- **BOT, Buoni Ordinari del Tesoro (IT: BOT)** — Italian zero-coupon government bills (3, 6, 12 months). Issued at discount. (→ sec. 13)
- **Breakeven inflation (IT: breakeven inflation)** — Gap between the nominal yield and the yield of an inflation-linked bond of the same maturity. Market proxy for expected inflation. (→ sec. 4)
- **Bretton Woods (IT: Bretton Woods)** — 1944 agreement that built the post-war monetary system on a dollar convertible to gold. Collapsed in 1971 with Nixon's suspension. (→ sec. 1)
- **BRRD, Bank Recovery and Resolution Directive (IT: BRRD)** — EU directive 2014/59 that introduced bail-in and the bank-resolution procedure. (→ sec. 30)
- **BTP, Buono del Tesoro Poliennale (IT: BTP)** — Italian medium-to-long-term government bond, semi-annual fixed coupon. Coupon taxed at 12.5%. (→ sec. 13)
- **BTP€i (IT: BTPi)** — Inflation-linked BTP indexed to EU HICP (ex-tobacco). Protects principal and coupons from inflation. (→ sec. 13)
- **Budget (IT: budget)** — Written plan of inflows and outflows over a period (usually monthly). Operational base of personal finance. (→ sec. 5)
- **Bund (IT: Bund)** — German 10-year government bond. Euro area risk-free benchmark. (→ sec. 13)

## C

- **Call (IT: call)** — Option granting the right (not obligation) to buy the underlying at a fixed strike on or before expiry. (→ sec. 23)
- **Calmar ratio (IT: Calmar ratio)** — Ratio of annualized return to maximum drawdown. Measures return per unit of pain. (→ sec. 21)
- **CAPM, Capital Asset Pricing Model (IT: CAPM)** — Model that explains expected return as risk-free rate plus a premium for systematic risk (beta). (→ sec. 19)
- **Carry trade (IT: carry trade)** — Strategy of borrowing in a low-rate currency to invest in a high-rate one, earning the rate differential. (→ sec. 4)
- **CCT (IT: CCT)** — Italian floating-rate government bond indexed to 6-month Euribor plus a spread. (→ sec. 13)
- **CDS, Credit Default Swap (IT: CDS)** — Derivative insuring against issuer default. Protection buyer pays a periodic premium and receives a payoff on the credit event. (→ sec. 22)
- **CET1, Common Equity Tier 1 (IT: CET1)** — Highest-quality bank capital: ordinary shares plus retained earnings. CET1 / RWA is the most-watched solvency ratio. (→ sec. 30)
- **Compounding (IT: compounding)** — Compound capitalization: accrued interest is reinvested and earns more interest. Foundation of exponential growth of capital. (→ sec. 11)
- **Consob (IT: Consob)** — Italian Securities and Exchange Commission. Supervises markets, approves prospectuses, sanctions abuses. (→ sec. 32)
- **Convexity (IT: convexity)** — Second-order sensitivity of bond price to yield changes. Corrects the linear duration approximation. (→ sec. 13)
- **Correlation (IT: correlazione)** — Statistical measure between –1 and +1 of how two variables move together. Zero doesn't imply independence — only no linear link. (→ sec. 18)
- **Cost-push inflation (IT: inflazione da costi)** — Inflation driven by rising input costs (energy, commodities, wages). Typical of oil shocks. (→ sec. 4)
- **Coupon (IT: cedola)** — Periodic interest payment of a bond, quoted as % of face value. (→ sec. 12)
- **CPI, Consumer Price Index (IT: CPI)** — Index of consumer prices. The EU harmonized version is HICP. (→ sec. 4)
- **CRIF (IT: CRIF)** — Leading Italian credit-bureau, runs the private "EURISC" registry. Your loans end up there. (→ sec. 8)
- **Crowdfunding (IT: crowdfunding)** — Raising capital from many small investors through an online platform. Equity, lending or reward models. EU-regulated by the ECSP Regulation. (→ sec. 40)
- **Cryptocurrency (IT: criptovaluta)** — Digital asset based on cryptography and distributed ledger. Bitcoin is the first and largest by market cap. (→ sec. 41)
- **CVaR, Conditional Value at Risk (IT: CVaR, Expected Shortfall)** — Average loss in the worst-case tail beyond the VaR threshold. More conservative than VaR and coherent as a risk measure. (→ sec. 21)

## D

- **DCA, Dollar Cost Averaging (IT: PAC)** — Strategy of investing a fixed amount at regular intervals regardless of price. Cuts timing error. (→ sec. 18)
- **DCF, Discounted Cash Flow (IT: DCF)** — Valuation method that discounts future cash flows at the WACC to estimate intrinsic value. (→ sec. 15)
- **Default (IT: default, inadempienza)** — Failure of a borrower to pay principal or interest on time. Triggers credit events. (→ sec. 12)
- **DeFi, Decentralized Finance (IT: DeFi)** — Set of financial protocols built on public blockchains (lending, exchange, derivatives) without traditional intermediaries. (→ sec. 41)
- **Deflation (IT: deflazione)** — General, persistent decline in prices. Dangerous because it encourages postponing purchases, suffocating demand. (→ sec. 4)
- **Delta (IT: delta)** — Greek: price change of the option per unit move of the underlying. Between 0 and 1 for calls, between –1 and 0 for puts. (→ sec. 23)
- **Demand-pull inflation (IT: inflazione da domanda)** — Inflation from excess aggregate demand vs supply. Typical of economic booms and aggressive fiscal stimulus. (→ sec. 4)
- **DEX, Decentralized Exchange (IT: DEX)** — Crypto exchange without a central operator, built on smart contracts. Uniswap is the canonical example. (→ sec. 41)
- **Diversification (IT: diversificazione)** — Spreading capital across many loosely correlated assets to reduce idiosyncratic risk. "The only free lunch in finance" — Markowitz. (→ sec. 18)
- **Dividend (IT: dividendo)** — Share of profits paid to stockholders. Cash or stock form (script dividend). In Italy taxed at 26%. (→ sec. 14)
- **Dollarization (IT: dollarizzazione)** — Adoption of the US dollar as legal tender in lieu of the national currency. Examples: Ecuador, El Salvador. (→ sec. 4)
- **Dot plot (IT: dot plot)** — Chart published by the Fed showing FOMC members' anonymous individual projections of future rates. (→ sec. 3)
- **Drawdown (IT: drawdown)** — Loss from the previous peak. The maximum drawdown (MaxDD) is the worst fall observed over the period. (→ sec. 21)
- **Duration (IT: duration)** — Bond price sensitivity to rates, in years. Macaulay duration = weighted average maturity of cash flows; modified = first derivative of price w.r.t. yield. (→ sec. 13)
- **DuPont analysis (IT: DuPont)** — Decomposition of ROE into three drivers: net margin, asset turnover, financial leverage. (→ sec. 15)

## E

- **EBA, European Banking Authority (IT: EBA)** — European banking authority based in Paris. Sets technical standards and runs the annual stress tests. (→ sec. 30)
- **EBITDA (IT: EBITDA)** — Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation and Amortization. Operating margin proxy for cash flow, controversial as it ignores capex and working-capital changes. (→ sec. 15)
- **ECB, European Central Bank (IT: BCE)** — Central bank of the euro area, based in Frankfurt. Sets policy rates and asset-purchase programmes. (→ sec. 3)
- **EIOPA (IT: EIOPA)** — European authority supervising insurance and pension funds. (→ sec. 32)
- **Equity (IT: equity, azioni)** — Ownership stake in a corporation; more broadly the stock asset class. (→ sec. 14)
- **ESG (IT: ESG)** — Environmental, Social, Governance. Sustainability criteria for evaluating investments. EU-regulated by SFDR. (→ sec. 40)
- **ESMA, European Securities and Markets Authority (IT: ESMA)** — European markets authority based in Paris. Supervises rating agencies, derivative repositories, EU prospectuses. (→ sec. 32)
- **ETF, Exchange Traded Fund (IT: ETF)** — Fund tracking an index, traded on exchange like a stock. Low cost, high liquidity. Not to be confused with EFT (Electronic Funds Transfer). (→ sec. 25)
- **EUR, Euro (IT: euro)** — Single currency of 20 EU countries (2026). Issued by the ECB. (→ sec. 1)
- **Euribor (IT: Euribor)** — Euro interbank offered rate, published from 1 week to 12 months. Reference for many Italian variable-rate mortgages. (→ sec. 11)
- **Eurosystem (IT: Eurosistema)** — ECB + national central banks of the euro area. Executes monetary policy decided by the Governing Council. (→ sec. 3)
- **EV, Enterprise Value (IT: EV)** — Market cap plus net debt. Measures firm value independent of capital structure. (→ sec. 15)
- **EV/EBITDA (IT: EV/EBITDA)** — Valuation multiple dividing enterprise value by EBITDA. Lets you compare companies with different leverage. (→ sec. 15)
- **Ex-dividend date (IT: stacco cedola)** — Date from which buyers of the stock are no longer entitled to the announced dividend. (→ sec. 14)

## F

- **Factor investing (IT: factor investing)** — Strategy based on systematic return factors (value, momentum, quality, low-vol, size). Extension of CAPM. (→ sec. 20)
- **Fama-French model (IT: Fama-French)** — 3-factor (later 5-factor) model extending CAPM with size (SMB) and value (HML). (→ sec. 20)
- **FCFE, Free Cash Flow to Equity (IT: FCFE)** — Cash available to shareholders after capex, working capital and debt changes. Discounted at cost of equity. (→ sec. 15)
- **FCFF, Free Cash Flow to Firm (IT: FCFF)** — Cash available to all capital providers (equity + debt). Discounted at WACC. (→ sec. 15)
- **Fed, Federal Reserve (IT: Fed)** — US central bank. Sets rates via the FOMC, meeting eight times per year. (→ sec. 3)
- **Fiat money (IT: moneta fiat)** — Legal tender not convertible into gold or another commodity, deriving value from trust in the issuing state. All modern currencies are fiat. (→ sec. 1)
- **Fintech (IT: fintech)** — Technology applied to finance, in particular startups offering traditionally-banking services with simpler UX. (→ sec. 40)
- **FITD, Italian Deposit Guarantee Fund (IT: FITD)** — Italian deposit protection scheme up to €100,000 per depositor per bank. (→ sec. 7)
- **FOMC, Federal Open Market Committee (IT: FOMC)** — Fed committee that sets interest rates and open-market operations. 12 voting members. (→ sec. 3)
- **Forward (IT: forward)** — OTC over-the-counter contract between two parties to exchange an asset at a future date and fixed price. Not standardized like a future. (→ sec. 22)
- **Forward guidance (IT: forward guidance)** — Central bank's explicit communication about future policy moves to anchor expectations. (→ sec. 3)
- **FSB, Financial Stability Board (IT: FSB)** — International body coordinating G20 financial stability policy. (→ sec. 32)
- **Futures contract (IT: future)** — Exchange-traded standardized contract to buy/sell an underlying at a future date and fixed price. Marked-to-market daily. (→ sec. 22)

## G

- **Gamma (IT: gamma)** — Greek: second derivative of option price w.r.t. underlying. Derivative of delta. Highest for ATM options. (→ sec. 23)
- **Gas (crypto) (IT: gas)** — Unit cost to execute operations on a blockchain (e.g. Ethereum), paid in the native token. Rises with congestion. (→ sec. 41)
- **GDP, Gross Domestic Product (IT: PIL)** — Value of all final goods and services produced in a country in a period. (→ sec. 4)
- **Gilt (IT: Gilt)** — British government bond. UK equivalent of the BTP. (→ sec. 13)
- **Gold standard (IT: gold standard)** — Monetary system where currency is convertible into gold at a fixed rate. Definitively abandoned in 1971. (→ sec. 1)
- **Gordon growth model (IT: Gordon growth)** — Valuation of a stock with constant dividend growth: $P = D_1 / (r - g)$. (→ sec. 15)
- **Greeks (IT: greche)** — Sensitivities of option prices to various parameters: delta, gamma, vega, theta, rho. Risk management tools for derivatives. (→ sec. 23)

## H

- **Halving (IT: halving)** — Halving of the Bitcoin block reward, programmed every 210,000 blocks (~4 years). Reduces BTC monetary inflation. (→ sec. 41)
- **Hard landing (IT: hard landing)** — Scenario where central-bank rate hikes trigger recession and unemployment. Opposite of soft landing. (→ sec. 4)
- **Hash (IT: hash)** — One-way cryptographic function producing a fixed-length fingerprint. Backbone of blockchain integrity. (→ sec. 41)
- **Hawkish (IT: hawkish)** — Central-bank stance leaning toward tightening (higher rates to fight inflation). Opposite: dovish. (→ sec. 3)
- **Helicopter money (IT: helicopter money)** — Direct distribution of central-bank money to citizens without creating public debt. Theoretical tool, never applied in pure form. (→ sec. 3)
- **Heston model (IT: Heston model)** — Option-pricing model with stochastic volatility, improving Black-Scholes. (→ sec. 23)
- **HICP, Harmonised Index of Consumer Prices (IT: HICP)** — EU-harmonized CPI version. ECB inflation target: 2% in the medium term. (→ sec. 4)
- **Hyperinflation (IT: iperinflazione)** — Explosive inflation (conventionally > 50% per month). Historical examples: Germany 1923, Zimbabwe 2008, Venezuela 2018. (→ sec. 4)

## I

- **IBAN (IT: IBAN)** — International Bank Account Number, unique cross-border account identifier. 27 chars in Italy, starting "IT". (→ sec. 7)
- **Impermanent loss (IT: impermanent loss)** — Opportunity loss of an AMM liquidity provider when the relative prices of the two assets diverge. (→ sec. 41)
- **IMU, Imposta Municipale Unica (IT: IMU)** — Italian property tax on non-primary residences. Base rate 0.86%, customizable by municipality. (→ sec. 36)
- **Inflation (IT: inflazione)** — General, persistent rise in the price level. Measured by CPI / HICP. (→ sec. 4)
- **Information ratio (IT: information ratio)** — Active return over benchmark divided by tracking error. Measures active-manager skill. (→ sec. 21)
- **INPS (IT: INPS)** — Italian national social security institute. Manages public pensions, unemployment (NASpI), maternity. (→ sec. 27)
- **IPO, Initial Public Offering (IT: IPO)** — First sale of shares by a company going public on an exchange. (→ sec. 14)
- **IRAP (IT: IRAP)** — Italian regional production tax. Base rate 3.9% of production value. (→ sec. 35)
- **IRES (IT: IRES)** — Italian corporate income tax. Rate 24%. (→ sec. 35)
- **IRP, Interest Rate Parity (IT: IRP)** — No-arbitrage relation between interest rates and forward FX: $F/S = (1+i_d)/(1+i_f)$. (→ sec. 4)
- **IRPEF (IT: IRPEF)** — Italian personal income tax. Progressive brackets: 23%, 35%, 43% (2026). (→ sec. 35)
- **IRR, Internal Rate of Return (IT: IRR, TIR)** — Discount rate that makes the NPV of a project zero. The "implicit" return of a cash-flow series. (→ sec. 11)
- **IRS, Interest Rate Swap (IT: IRS)** — Rate swap: two counterparties exchange fixed for floating cash flows on the same notional. Used to hedge variable-rate mortgages. (→ sec. 11)
- **ISC, Indicatore Sintetico di Costo (IT: ISC)** — Italian equivalent of APR for bank accounts, expressed in €/year. Mandatory in the fact sheet. (→ sec. 7)
- **VAT (IT: IVA)** — Value Added Tax. Italian standard rate 22%; reduced rates 10%, 5%, 4%. (→ sec. 35)
- **IVAFE (IT: IVAFE)** — Italian tax on financial assets held abroad. 0.20% of value (fixed €34.20 for deposits). Reported in schedule RW. (→ sec. 36)
- **IVASS (IT: IVASS)** — Italian Insurance Supervisory Authority. (→ sec. 32)

## J

- **Jensen's alpha (IT: alfa di Jensen)** — Risk-adjusted performance: excess return vs CAPM prediction. (→ sec. 21)
- **JOMO, Joy of Missing Out (IT: JOMO)** — In personal finance: peace of mind from not chasing every new investment trend. Counterpart to FOMO. (→ sec. 37)

## K

- **KID, Key Information Document (IT: KID)** — Standard EU 3-page summary for retail investment products (PRIIPs). (→ sec. 25)
- **KIID, Key Investor Information Document (IT: KIID)** — Predecessor of KID for UCITS funds. Replaced by KID from 2023. (→ sec. 25)
- **KYC, Know Your Customer (IT: KYC)** — Customer identity verification by the intermediary. Required by anti-money-laundering rules. (→ sec. 32)

## L

- **LCR, Liquidity Coverage Ratio (IT: LCR)** — Basel III regulatory ratio: 30-day stressed liquidity coverage. ≥ 100%. (→ sec. 30)
- **Lender of last resort (IT: prestatore di ultima istanza)** — Central bank's role in providing emergency liquidity to solvent but illiquid banks. (→ sec. 3)
- **Leverage (IT: leva)** — Debt-to-equity ratio. Amplifies both returns and losses. (→ sec. 22)
- **Leverage ratio (IT: leverage ratio)** — Basel III regulatory ratio: Tier 1 / total unweighted exposure. Minimum 3%. (→ sec. 30)
- **LIBOR — deprecated (IT: LIBOR)** — London Interbank Offered Rate, historic dollar-lending benchmark. Replaced by SOFR from 2023 after a manipulation scandal. (→ sec. 11)
- **Liquidity pool (IT: liquidity pool)** — Token reserve locked in a smart contract used by an AMM to quote swaps. (→ sec. 41)
- **Lock-up (IT: lock-up)** — Period (typically 90–180 days) during which insiders and pre-IPO holders cannot sell their shares. (→ sec. 14)
- **Log-return (IT: log-rendimento)** — Logarithmic return: $\ln(P_t / P_{t-1})$. Time-additive, ideal for statistical analysis. (→ sec. 19)
- **Lower bound, ZLB (IT: lower bound)** — Implicit floor on nominal rates. Below zero (negative) brings practical frictions for central banks. (→ sec. 3)

## M

- **M0, M1, M2, M3 (IT: M0, M1, M2, M3)** — Monetary aggregates of increasing breadth: from monetary base (M0) to various deposits + near-money instruments. (→ sec. 1)
- **M-squared, Modigliani measure (IT: M²)** — Reformulation of Sharpe ratio in % return terms of a portfolio "calibrated" to market volatility. (→ sec. 21)
- **MaxDD, Maximum Drawdown (IT: MaxDD)** — Worst loss from previous peak over the observed period. Intuitive risk measure. (→ sec. 21)
- **ESM, European Stability Mechanism (IT: MES)** — Euro area bailout fund, capital €700bn. (→ sec. 32)
- **MiCA, Markets in Crypto-Assets (IT: MiCA)** — EU Regulation 2023/1114 on crypto-assets, fully applicable from 2024-25. (→ sec. 41)
- **MiFID II (IT: MiFID II)** — EU Directive 2014/65 on financial instrument markets, mandating client profiling and cost disclosure. (→ sec. 25)
- **Momentum (IT: momentum)** — Carhart's add-on factor: securities that performed best in the past 6–12 months tend to keep outperforming. (→ sec. 20)
- **Money-market fund (IT: fondo monetario)** — Fund investing in short-term money-market instruments (T-bills, deposits). Low risk, low return. (→ sec. 25)
- **Monte Carlo simulation (IT: Monte Carlo)** — Pricing technique that simulates thousands of random scenarios to estimate the expected value of a derivative or portfolio. (→ sec. 23)
- **Mortgage (IT: mutuo)** — Loan secured by a mortgage lien on real estate. In Italy usually under the French amortization schedule. (→ sec. 8)
- **MPT, Modern Portfolio Theory (IT: MPT)** — Markowitz's 1952 theory: optimal diversification minimizes variance for a given expected return. (→ sec. 18)
- **MWR, Money-Weighted Return (IT: MWR)** — IRR of the investor's cash flows. Captures timing of contributions and withdrawals. (→ sec. 21)

## N

- **NAV, Net Asset Value (IT: NAV)** — Net asset value per share of a fund. Computed daily (sometimes weekly for alternatives). (→ sec. 25)
- **NEER, Nominal Effective Exchange Rate (IT: NEER)** — Weighted average of bilateral exchange rates with main trading partners. (→ sec. 4)
- **Neobank (IT: neobank)** — 100% digital bank, no physical branches. Examples: Revolut, N26, Hype. (→ sec. 40)
- **Neutral rate, R\* (IT: R\* neutral rate)** — Real equilibrium rate: neither stimulative nor restrictive. Constantly re-estimated by central banks. (→ sec. 3)
- **NFT, Non-Fungible Token (IT: NFT)** — Unique blockchain token representing ownership of an asset (digital or physical). Not interchangeable one-for-one. (→ sec. 41)
- **NPL, Non-Performing Loan (IT: NPL)** — Bank loan whose borrower is impaired or in default. Subset of NPE. (→ sec. 30)
- **NPE, Non-Performing Exposure (IT: NPE)** — Overall impaired exposure (NPL + other forms). Bank-credit-health gauge. (→ sec. 30)
- **NPV, Net Present Value (IT: NPV, VAN)** — Sum of discounted future cash flows minus initial investment. NPV > 0 → value-creating project. (→ sec. 11)
- **NSFR, Net Stable Funding Ratio (IT: NSFR)** — Basel III ratio of available stable funding to required stable funding over one year. ≥ 100%. (→ sec. 30)

## O

- **OIS, Overnight Index Swap (IT: OIS)** — Swap exchanging fixed for the geometric average of overnight rates. Proxy for risk-free rate. (→ sec. 22)
- **OPEC+ (IT: OPEC+)** — Cartel of oil-exporting countries (OPEC + Russia and others). Influences crude prices. (→ sec. 4)
- **Open banking (IT: open banking)** — Opening of bank data to fintech via API with customer consent. In EU regulated by PSD2. (→ sec. 40)
- **Option (IT: opzione)** — Derivative granting the buyer the right (not obligation) to buy (call) or sell (put) the underlying. (→ sec. 23)
- **OTC, Over-The-Counter (IT: OTC)** — Off-exchange market: bilateral contracts between two parties, not standardized and not centrally cleared. (→ sec. 22)

## P

- **PCE, Personal Consumption Expenditures (IT: PCE)** — US personal consumption deflator, preferred inflation gauge of the Fed. (→ sec. 4)
- **P/B, Price-to-Book (IT: P/B)** — Multiple: share price divided by book value per share. Useful for banks, distorted for tech with few tangible assets. (→ sec. 15)
- **P/E, Price-to-Earnings (IT: P/E)** — Classic multiple: share price divided by EPS. High P/E = growth expectations or expensive stock. (→ sec. 15)
- **PEG, Price/Earnings to Growth (IT: PEG)** — P/E divided by expected earnings growth. PEG ~ 1 → fair price for growth. (→ sec. 15)
- **Phillips curve (IT: curva di Phillips)** — Empirical negative relation between inflation and unemployment. Revised post-1970s once expectations were added. (→ sec. 4)
- **PIP, Italian Individual Pension Plan (IT: PIP)** — Italian personal pension plan, usually distributed by insurers. (→ sec. 27)
- **PIR, Italian Individual Savings Plan (IT: PIR)** — Italian fiscal wrapper exempting gains from tax if held ≥ 5 years. (→ sec. 36)
- **PMI, Purchasing Managers' Index (IT: PMI indicatore)** — Monthly survey of purchasing managers: PMI > 50 = expansion, < 50 = contraction. (→ sec. 4)
- **PPP, Purchasing Power Parity (IT: PPP)** — Long-run exchange-rate theory based on relative price levels across countries. (→ sec. 4)
- **PRIIP (IT: PRIIP)** — Packaged Retail and Insurance-based Investment Products. Category to which KID applies. (→ sec. 25)
- **Proof-of-Stake, PoS (IT: PoS)** — Blockchain consensus mechanism where validators are picked based on staked tokens. More energy-efficient than PoW. (→ sec. 41)
- **Proof-of-Work, PoW (IT: PoW)** — Consensus mechanism requiring costly computation. Used by Bitcoin, abandoned by Ethereum in 2022. (→ sec. 41)
- **PSD2, Payment Services Directive 2 (IT: PSD2)** — EU Directive 2015/2366 that opened bank accounts to fintech via API. (→ sec. 40)
- **Put (IT: put)** — Option granting the right to sell the underlying at a fixed strike. Used to hedge downside. (→ sec. 23)
- **Put-call parity (IT: put-call parity)** — No-arbitrage relation between European calls and puts with same strike/expiry: $C - P = S - K e^{-rT}$. (→ sec. 23)

## Q

- **QE, Quantitative Easing (IT: QE)** — Large-scale asset purchases by the central bank to inject liquidity. (→ sec. 3)
- **QT, Quantitative Tightening (IT: QT)** — Reverse of QE: the central bank lets bonds mature without reinvesting (or sells actively) to drain liquidity. (→ sec. 3)
- **RW schedule, Quadro RW (IT: quadro RW)** — Section of the Italian tax return for declaring foreign-held financial assets. Required also for crypto. (→ sec. 36)

## R

- **Branches I, III, V — Italian life policies (IT: Ramo I, III, V)** — Italian life-insurance categories: I revaluable in segregated account; III unit/index-linked; V capitalization. (→ sec. 28)
- **Real interest rate (IT: tasso reale)** — Real rate: nominal rate minus expected inflation (Fisher equation). (→ sec. 4)
- **REER, Real Effective Exchange Rate (IT: REER)** — NEER adjusted for inflation differentials. (→ sec. 4)
- **Managed regime (IT: regime amministrato)** — Italian tax regime where the bank/SIM acts as withholding agent. (→ sec. 36)
- **Declarative regime (IT: regime dichiarativo)** — Italian tax regime where the investor reports gains/losses in schedule RT (required for foreign brokers). (→ sec. 36)
- **Discretionary regime (IT: regime gestito)** — Italian tax regime for managed portfolios, taxed on accrual basis on the result of the mandate. (→ sec. 36)
- **REIT, SIIQ (IT: REIT, SIIQ)** — Real Estate Investment Trust: listed company investing in real estate and distributing ≥ 90% of profits. Italian version: SIIQ. (→ sec. 26)
- **Return (IT: rendimento)** — Percentage change in the value of an investment over a period. Can be gross or net. (→ sec. 18)
- **Repo (IT: repo)** — Repurchase agreement: sale of securities with repurchase commitment. Equivalent to a collateralized loan. (→ sec. 3)
- **Reverse repo (IT: reverse repo)** — Other side of the repo: temporary purchase of securities. Drains liquidity from the system. (→ sec. 3)
- **Rho (IT: rho)** — Greek: option price sensitivity to interest rate. (→ sec. 23)
- **Risk-free rate (IT: tasso risk-free)** — Yield of a "risk-free" bond (typically US Treasury or German Bund). Base of CAPM. (→ sec. 19)
- **ROA, Return on Assets (IT: ROA)** — Net income / total assets. Measures efficiency in using assets. (→ sec. 15)
- **Robo-advisor (IT: robo-advisor)** — Automated portfolio management service based on algorithms and online profiling. (→ sec. 40)
- **ROE, Return on Equity (IT: ROE)** — Net income / shareholders' equity. Return for shareholders. Decomposable via DuPont. (→ sec. 15)
- **ROIC, Return on Invested Capital (IT: ROIC)** — Net operating profit (NOPAT) / invested capital. Business-quality indicator. (→ sec. 15)
- **RWA, Risk-Weighted Assets (IT: RWA)** — Bank assets weighted by risk. Denominator of capital ratios. (→ sec. 30)

## S

- **Sharpe ratio (IT: Sharpe ratio)** — $(R_p - R_f) / \sigma_p$: excess return per unit of total volatility. Most popular risk/return measure. (→ sec. 21)
- **SFDR, Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (IT: SFDR)** — EU Regulation 2019/2088 on ESG disclosure for financial products. Article 6, 8, 9 classification. (→ sec. 40)
- **SICAV (IT: SICAV)** — Société d'Investissement à Capital Variable: European legal form of open-ended fund. (→ sec. 25)
- **SIM, Italian investment firm (IT: SIM)** — Italian intermediary licensed to trade financial instruments on behalf of clients. (→ sec. 32)
- **Smart contract (IT: smart contract)** — Program executed automatically on blockchain when predefined conditions are met. (→ sec. 41)
- **SOFR, Secured Overnight Financing Rate (IT: SOFR)** — US overnight rate secured by Treasury repo. Successor of LIBOR USD. (→ sec. 11)
- **Soft landing (IT: soft landing)** — Scenario where the central bank tames inflation without causing recession. Historically rare. (→ sec. 4)
- **Sortino ratio (IT: Sortino ratio)** — Sharpe variant using only the downside deviation of returns. (→ sec. 21)
- **Spread (IT: spread)** — Gap between two quantities: rates (BTP-Bund), bid-ask prices, credit risk. (→ sec. 13)
- **Stablecoin (IT: stablecoin)** — Cryptocurrency pegged to a stable asset (usually USD). Types: fiat-collateralized, crypto-collateralized, algorithmic. (→ sec. 41)
- **Stagflation (IT: stagflation)** — Coexistence of high inflation and stagnation/recession. Historic example: US 1970s. (→ sec. 4)
- **Stress test (IT: stress test)** — Simulation of a portfolio/bank under extreme adverse scenarios. EBA runs biennial stress tests. (→ sec. 30)
- **SWIFT (IT: SWIFT)** — Global interbank messaging network for payment orders. Synonym of BIC. (→ sec. 7)

## T

- **APR, TAEG (IT: TAEG)** — Annual Percentage Rate of a loan: includes interest, fees and mandatory insurance. Mandatory disclosure. (→ sec. 8)
- **Nominal interest rate, TAN (IT: TAN)** — "Pure" interest of a loan without ancillary fees. Lower than APR. (→ sec. 8)
- **Tapering (IT: taper)** — Gradual slowdown of QE purchases. Famous for the 2013 "taper tantrum". (→ sec. 3)
- **TER, Total Expense Ratio (IT: TER)** — Total annual cost of a fund/ETF expressed in % of AUM. (→ sec. 25)
- **TFR, Italian severance pay (IT: TFR)** — Accrued severance for Italian employees, paid out at the end of employment. Can be diverted to a pension fund. (→ sec. 27)
- **Theta (IT: theta)** — Greek: time decay of an option's price. Negative for option buyers. (→ sec. 23)
- **TIPS / BTPi (IT: TIPS / BTPi)** — Inflation-linked government bonds. US version: TIPS; Italian: BTP€i. (→ sec. 13)
- **Treasury (IT: Treasury)** — US government bond. T-bill (≤1 year), T-note (2-10), T-bond (>10). (→ sec. 13)
- **Treynor ratio (IT: Treynor ratio)** — Sharpe variant using beta instead of total volatility. Premium for systematic risk. (→ sec. 21)
- **Mundell-Fleming trilemma (IT: trilemma di Mundell)** — A country cannot simultaneously have: fixed FX, free capital mobility, autonomous monetary policy. Only two of three. (→ sec. 4)
- **TWR, Time-Weighted Return (IT: TWR)** — Return "cleaned" of investor cash flows. Measures the manager's performance. (→ sec. 21)

## U

- **UCITS (IT: UCITS)** — Undertakings for Collective Investment in Transferable Securities. EU-harmonized standard for retail funds. (→ sec. 25)
- **Underwriting (IT: underwriting)** — Subscription/placement of a security issue by an investment bank. (→ sec. 14)
- **Unwinding (IT: unwinding)** — Gradual unwinding of a position (e.g. central banks shrinking the QE balance sheet). (→ sec. 3)

## V

- **VaR, Value at Risk (IT: VaR)** — Maximum expected loss over a horizon at a given confidence level. E.g. "VaR 1d 99% = €1m". (→ sec. 21)
- **Historical VaR (IT: VaR storico)** — VaR computed as the empirical quantile of past returns. Non-parametric. (→ sec. 21)
- **Parametric VaR (IT: VaR parametrico)** — VaR computed assuming a normal distribution: $-z_\alpha \cdot \sigma$. (→ sec. 21)
- **Vega (IT: vega)** — Greek: option price sensitivity to implied volatility. Highest for ATM long-dated options. (→ sec. 23)
- **Volatility (IT: volatilità)** — Standard deviation of returns. For financial assets typically annualized: $\sigma_y = \sigma_d \sqrt{252}$. (→ sec. 19)

## W

- **W-8BEN (IT: W-8BEN)** — IRS form signed by non-US residents to claim the treaty rate on US income (e.g. US dividends at 15% instead of 30%). (→ sec. 36)
- **WACC, Weighted Average Cost of Capital (IT: WACC)** — Weighted average of after-tax cost of debt and cost of equity. Discount rate for DCF. (→ sec. 15)
- **World Bank (IT: Banca Mondiale)** — Bretton Woods institution focused on development loans for low/middle-income countries. (→ sec. 32)

## X

- **XAU/USD (IT: XAU/USD)** — ISO code for the gold ounce price in dollars, used on Forex. (→ sec. 17)
- **XBT (IT: XBT)** — Unofficial ISO code for Bitcoin (alternative to "BTC"). (→ sec. 41)

## Y

- **Yield (IT: rendimento, yield)** — Bond return, expressed as YTM (yield-to-maturity) or current yield. (→ sec. 12)
- **YTM, Yield to Maturity (IT: YTM)** — Internal rate of return of a bond held to maturity, assuming coupons are reinvested at the same rate. (→ sec. 12)

## Z

- **ZEW (IT: ZEW)** — German financial-analyst sentiment indicator, monthly, published by the ZEW Mannheim institute. (→ sec. 4)
- **ZLB, Zero Lower Bound (IT: ZLB)** — Zero floor on nominal rates. Central banks have tested even negative rates (ECB 2014-2022, Switzerland). (→ sec. 3)
- **Zombie firm (IT: zombie firm)** — Company whose operating profit is insufficient to cover interest expenses, surviving only thanks to low rates or continuous restructurings. (→ sec. 4)
