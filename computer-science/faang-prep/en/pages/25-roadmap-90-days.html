90-day Roadmap — operational plan

Daily plan for 13 weeks (~2h/day). From theory to mocks. Exact list of problems for each week.

Area
Strategy

The plan below assumes ~2h/day for 13 weeks (~91 days). Adapt to your time: if you have 1h/day, double the duration.

Abbreviations: L = LeetCode, D = difficulty, P = day's problems.

Philosophy

  • Patterns > volume. 5 problems of the same category > 5 random problems.
  • Time-boxing. Easy 15 min, Medium 25 min, Hard 40 min. If exceeded, read the solution.
  • One weekly revision. Every Sunday, redo 5 old problems.
  • Mocks from day 50 onwards. Start early, don't wait to be "ready".

Initial setup (days 1-3)

  • Open LeetCode account (premium recommended after first 30 days if serious).
  • Install an editor with Python.
  • Create a Google Sheet "FAANG log" with: date, problem, difficulty, time spent, result (alone / hint / solution), pattern.
  • Print ch. 22 — Coding Patterns.

By the end of 13 weeks you should have ~150-180 problems solved.

Checklist "ready for the loop"

  • [ ] 150+ problems solved, of which ≥ 75 medium and ≥ 20 hard.
  • [ ] You solve a random medium in ≤ 25 min 70% of the time.
  • [ ] You can describe 3+ systems end-to-end out loud.
  • [ ] You have 10 STAR stories written and rehearsed.
  • [ ] You've done at least 5 total mock interviews.
  • [ ] You can declare time + space complexity for every solution.
  • [ ] You know the 16 coding patterns (chapter 22) by heart.

When you can check all boxes: apply. Don't wait to feel "completely ready" — that moment never arrives. The loop itself will teach you new things.

Good luck.
