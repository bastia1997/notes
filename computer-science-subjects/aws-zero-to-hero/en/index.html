Become an expert in
Amazon Web Services

From "what is a Region" all the way to designing a multi-account, multi-region, event-driven architecture with security guardrails and FinOps. A long, ordered path, in English, covering networking, compute, storage, databases, serverless, containers, ML, security, DevOps, migrations and the Well-Architected Framework. University-grade and field-grade material made chewable: every service comes with intuition, mental model, hands-on examples with CLI commands, exercises with hidden answers, and — where it matters — the anti-patterns that blow up your bill or open public buckets.

52 sections Bilingual IT · EN CLI + IaC, hands-on Networking · Compute · Data Security · DevOps · ML

How to use

Sections follow a logical order: each chapter builds on the previous. You can jump to what you need (e.g. straight to "DynamoDB" if you're modeling a table), but if you want to actually understand why a "stateful" security group is not an ACL, read the VPC section first. Every page contains:

  • Mental model — how the service behaves under the hood (cell-based architecture, control plane vs data plane, eventual consistency, etc.).
  • Real commands — AWS CLI snippets, CloudFormation, Terraform, AWS CDK. No console-only screenshots.
  • Charts — Mermaid diagrams and inline SVG, no ASCII art.
  • Exercises and scenarios — "what happens if…", questions with hidden answers, mini-labs.
  • Anti-patterns and gotchas — expensive egress, $90/mo NAT Gateways doing nothing, IAM with *, public S3, etc.

Philosophy

AWS is not "200 services to memorize". It is a platform of composable primitives: compute (EC2/Lambda/Fargate), storage (EBS/EFS/S3), networking (VPC/PrivateLink), identity (IAM/STS), events (SNS/SQS/EventBridge). Everything else is a combination of those primitives packaged a specific way. If you understand the primitives — when a request hits the control plane vs the data plane, when you pay per-request vs per-hour vs per-GB-month, where an IAM principal lives — the 200 services become variations on a theme, not 200 new things.

No "lift and shift your servers to the cloud and call it a day". No "70% savings" promises without modeling the workload. Just the conceptual machinery and hands-on practice to design, deploy and operate AWS systems so they don't cost you a fortune, don't break at 3am, and don't end up on the news for a breach.

Who it's for

  • Anyone starting from zero (including from on-prem or another cloud) who wants an ordered path without skipping fundamentals.
  • Anyone who knows a piece (e.g. "I use EC2 and RDS") but has big gaps (e.g. "I don't know what a VPC endpoint or a placement group is").
  • Anyone about to make an architectural call (multi-region, DB choice, container vs Lambda) who wants to understand the trade-offs before committing.
  • Anyone preparing for AWS certifications: CCP, SAA, DVA, SOA, SAP, DOP, ANS, SCS, MLS, DBS, DAS.

Who it's NOT for

  • Anyone looking for "copy-paste recipes for production" without wanting to understand. AWS punishes cargo cult: blindly copying an online tutorial can open public S3 buckets or leave a NAT Gateway running at $200/mo.
  • Anyone wanting console clicks only. Here CLI and IaC are the native language: 80% of serious shops deploy with CloudFormation/Terraform/CDK.

Recommended order

Follow sections in order. Estimated time for the full path: 3–6 months at 30–60 minutes per day plus hands-on labs. It's not a race. "Reference" chapters (certifications roadmap, A-Z glossary, services + limits cheatsheet) are meant to be consulted back and forth at will.
