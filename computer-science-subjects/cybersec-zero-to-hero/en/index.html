A complete path, in English, starting from computing fundamentals and reaching red team, reverse engineering, cloud, AI security and adversary emulation. University-level theory made chewable, hundreds of exercises, real labs.

How to use

Sections follow a logical order: each chapter builds bricks on the previous one. Don't skip the fundamentals even if you already know something: security is a chain, and you break it at its weakest link. Every page has three parts:

  • Theory — the how and why. No magic: understand the mechanisms.
  • Examples & labs — commands, code, scenarios to reproduce in a VM.
  • Exercises — questions with hidden solutions, practical challenges, links to real CTFs.

What you need to start

  • A PC with at least 16 GB of RAM (8 GB will do, you'll be limited on VMs).
  • VirtualBox or VMware Workstation Player (free).
  • A Kali Linux or Parrot OS VM for offensive work; a clean Ubuntu or Debian as target.
  • Free accounts on TryHackMe, HackTheBox, OverTheWire, PortSwigger Web Security Academy.
  • Curiosity. The rest is time.

Ethics & law — read before touching anything

In Italy, unauthorized access to a computer system is a crime (art. 615-ter c.p.). In the US it's the CFAA, in the UK the Computer Misuse Act. Good faith is not a defense. "I was just looking" is not a defense.

All techniques described here must be used on:

  1. Systems that you own (your VM, your router, your server).
  2. Training platforms (HTB, THM, root-me, picoCTF, ...).
  3. Bug bounty programs with explicit scope (HackerOne, Bugcrowd, Intigriti, YesWeHack).
  4. Professional engagements with a written contract (signed rules of engagement).
