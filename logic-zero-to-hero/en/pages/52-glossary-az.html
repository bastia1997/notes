A-Z bilingual glossary

Main course terms in alphabetical order with concise definition and section link. Italian term in italics after the English.

Each entry: English term, Italian term in italics, brief definition, section link.

A

Abduction (abduzione). Inference to the best explanation. From effect to most plausible cause. Section 03.

Ad hominem. Fallacy of attacking the person instead of the argument. Three sub-types. Section 21.

Anchoring. Bias: an initial value, even irrelevant, shifts later estimates. Section 23.

Antifragile. System that benefits from shocks and stress. Taleb 2012. Section 37.

Argument (argomento). Set of premises supporting a conclusion. Section 04.

Axiom (assioma). Undemonstrated premise, starting point of a formal system. Section 11.

B

BATNA. Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement. Section 41.

Bayes' theorem. $P(A|B) = P(B|A)P(A)/P(B)$. Updates beliefs with evidence. Section 33.

Biconditional (biconcizionale). $p \leftrightarrow q$: true when $p, q$ have same truth value. Section 07.

Black swan. Rare, high-impact, retrospectively explainable event. Taleb. Section 37.

Brier score. Metric of probabilistic forecast quality: $\frac{1}{N}\sum(p_i - o_i)^2$. Section 36.

C

Calibration. Forecaster is calibrated if "70%" occurs ~70% of times. Section 36.

Causality (causalità). Asymmetric productive relation: $X$ causes $Y$ if intervention on $X$ alters $Y$. Section 45.

CNF. Conjunctive Normal Form: conjunction of disjunctions of literals. Section 08.

Cognitive bias. Systematic judgment distortion. Section 23.

Common knowledge. Everyone knows, everyone knows that everyone knows, ad infinitum. Section 17.

Confounder. Variable causing both $X$ and $Y$, creating spurious correlation. Section 45.

Confirmation bias. Seeking/remembering information that confirms one's view. Section 23.

Conjunction fallacy. Mistake: $P(A \wedge B) > P(A)$. Linda example. Section 23.

Contradiction (contraddizione). Always-false formula. Section 07.

Contrapositive. From $p \rightarrow q$ to $\neg q \rightarrow \neg p$. Logically equivalent. Section 08.

Counterfactual (controfattuale). "Had $X$ not occurred, would $Y$?". Third rung of Pearl's ladder. Section 45.

Curry-Howard. Propositions as types, proofs as programs. Section 19.

D

DAG. Directed Acyclic Graph. Used to model causality. Section 45.

De Morgan's laws. $\neg(p \wedge q) \equiv \neg p \vee \neg q$ and dual. Section 08.

Deduction (deduzione). From general to particular; validity-preserving. Section 03.

Natural deduction. Proof system with I/E rules per connective. Section 10.

Demarcation. Criterion separating science from non-science. Popper: falsifiability. Section 43.

Deontic. Modal logic for obligations and permissions. Section 17.

Design thinking. User-centered problem solving methodology: 5 phases. Section 30.

Dialectic (dialettica). Socratic method: question-driven discovery. Section 40.

Disjunction (disgiunzione). $p \vee q$. Section 07.

Dual process. Mind as two systems: S1 (fast), S2 (slow). Section 24.

Dunning-Kruger. Overestimation of competence in the least competent. Section 23.

E

Endowment effect. Overvalue what you own. Section 23.

Epistemology. Study of knowledge. Section 42.

Equivocation. Same term, two meanings, in one argument. Section 22.

Heuristic. Mental shortcut, efficient but sometimes fallacious. Section 26.

Exercises. Section 51.

F

Fallacy. Argument seeming valid but isn't. Formal or informal. Sections 20-22.

Falsifiability. A theory is scientific if confutable by observation in principle. Popper. Section 43.

FOL. First-Order Logic: $\forall, \exists$ over individuals. Section 12, 13.

Forecasting. Probabilistic prediction. Section 36.

Framing. Same problem, different descriptions, different choices. Section 35.

Frequentism. Probability as long-run frequency. Section 32.

Fuzzy logic. Logic with values in $[0,1]$ (Zadeh). Section 18.

G

Gettier problem. JTB doesn't suffice for knowledge (1963). Section 42.

Gödel's theorems. First: PA-extending consistent systems have true unprovable sentences. Second: such systems can't prove their own consistency. Section 15.

Glossary. This section, 52.

H

Hindsight bias. Retrospective conviction outcome was predictable. Section 23.

Hume's problem of induction. Induction has no rational justification — habit. Section 43.

I

Implication (implicazione). $p \rightarrow q$. Section 07.

Incommensurability. Different paradigms share no common language (Kuhn). Section 44.

Induction. Generalize from observed cases. Section 03. Also a proof method.

Inference. Movement from premises to conclusion. Section 09.

Intuitionistic logic. Constructive: rejects excluded middle. Section 18.

J

JTB. Justified True Belief. Classical pre-Gettier theory. Section 42.

K

Knightian uncertainty. Uncertainty with unknown probabilities. Section 37.

Kripke semantics. Modal semantics via possible worlds. Section 16.

Kuhn paradigm. Shared framework within which normal science operates. Section 44.

L

Lateral thinking. Non-linear, generative problem solving (de Bono). Section 29.

Likelihood. $P(\text{evidence} | \text{hypothesis})$. Section 33.

Logos. (1) Greek "reason, word". (2) Rational rhetorical appeal. Section 39.

Loss aversion. Loss weighs ~2× equivalent gain. Section 35.

M

Mental model. Conceptual schema for reasoning in a domain. Section 31.

Metalogic. Study of logical systems themselves. Section 15.

Modus ponens. $p \rightarrow q, p \vdash q$. Section 09.

Modus tollens. $p \rightarrow q, \neg q \vdash \neg p$. Section 09.

Monty Hall. Probability paradox: always switch. Section 34.

N

Nash equilibrium. Strategic configuration from which no one improves by unilateral change. Section 41.

Negation (negazione). $\neg p$. Section 07.

O

Occam's razor. Prefer the simplest hypothesis that fits data. Section 31.

OODA loop. Observe-Orient-Decide-Act (Boyd). Section 25.

P

Paradox. Apparently valid argument leading to absurd conclusion. Section 46.

Paraconsistent. Tolerates contradictions without explosion. Section 18.

Pearl do-calculus. Formal system for causal inference. Section 45.

Computational thinking. Decomposition + pattern + abstraction + algorithms. Section 27.

Polya (4 problem-solving steps). Understand, plan, execute, look back. Section 25.

Popper (falsificationism). Section 43.

Predicate (predicato). Property or relation applied to individuals. Section 12.

Premise (premessa). Argument's starting assumption. Section 04.

Principle of charity. Interpret others as rationally as possible. Section 40.

Prior, posterior. Belief before/after evidence (Bayes). Section 33.

Problem solving. Sections 25-28.

Proposition (proposizione). Declarative statement with truth value. Section 07.

Prospect theory. Descriptive decision theory: reference, loss aversion, framing. Section 35.

Q

Quantifier (quantificatore). $\forall$ (universal), $\exists$ (existential). Section 12.

R

Reasoning (ragionamento). Drawing conclusions from premises. Section 03.

RCT. Randomized Controlled Trial. Gold standard for causation. Section 45.

Regression to the mean. Extreme measurements tend toward the average. Section 31.

Rhetoric (retorica). Art of persuasion. Ethos, pathos, logos. Section 39.

Risk vs uncertainty. Section 37.

Russell's paradox. Set of sets not containing themselves. Section 46.

S

S1, S2. System 1 and System 2 (Kahneman). Section 24.

SCAMPER. Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other use, Eliminate, Reverse. Section 29.

Semantics (semantica). Study of meaning of formulas. Section 13.

Sequent (sequente). $\Gamma \vdash \Delta$. Section 11.

SIFT. Stop, Investigate, Find, Trace — news literacy. Section 50.

Syllogism (sillogismo). Aristotelian two-premise argument. Section 02.

Simpson's paradox. Trend reverses under aggregation. Section 34.

Slippery slope. Fallacy of unjustified causal chain. Section 21.

Sorites paradox. Heap paradox. Section 46.

Soundness. $\vdash \Rightarrow \models$ (derivations preserve truth). Section 15.

Steelman. Reconstruct opponent's argument in strongest form. Section 40.

Straw man. Fallacy of caricaturing opponent. Section 21.

Sunk cost fallacy. Continuing to "not waste what's spent". Section 23.

Systems thinking. Section 47.

T

Truth table. Enumeration of T/F assignments. Section 07.

Tautology. Always-true formula. Section 07.

Game theory. Formal analysis of strategic interactions. Section 41.

Tit-for-tat. Winning strategy in repeated games. Section 41.

Toulmin model. Claim, data, warrant, backing, qualifier, rebuttal. Section 38.

Type theory. Foundational alternative to set theory via Curry-Howard. Section 19.

U

Expected utility. $\mathbb{E}[u(X)]$. Normative criterion of rational decision. Section 35.

V

Validity (validità). Argument property: if premises true, conclusion true. Section 04.

VUCA. Volatility, Uncertainty, Complexity, Ambiguity. Section 48.

Epistemic virtue. Cognitive trait for knowledge: open-mindedness, humility. Section 42.

von Neumann-Morgenstern. Axiomatic basis of expected utility. Section 35.

W

Warrant. Implicit justification in a Toulmin argument. Section 38.

Wicked problem. Problem without shared definition or "right" solution. Section 48.

Z

Zeno's paradoxes. Achilles & tortoise, arrow, dichotomy. Section 46.

ZOPA. Zone Of Possible Agreement. Section 41.
