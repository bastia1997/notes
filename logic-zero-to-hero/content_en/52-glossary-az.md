# A-Z bilingual glossary

Each entry: English term, *Italian term* in italics, brief definition, section link.

## A

**Abduction** *(abduzione)*. Inference to the best explanation. From effect to most plausible cause. Section [03](03-types-of-reasoning.html).

**Ad hominem**. Fallacy of attacking the person instead of the argument. Three sub-types. Section [21](21-informal-fallacies-relevance.html).

**Anchoring**. Bias: an initial value, even irrelevant, shifts later estimates. Section [23](23-cognitive-biases.html).

**Antifragile**. System that benefits from shocks and stress. Taleb 2012. Section [37](37-knightian-black-swans.html).

**Argument** *(argomento)*. Set of premises supporting a conclusion. Section [04](04-anatomy-of-arguments.html).

**Axiom** *(assioma)*. Undemonstrated premise, starting point of a formal system. Section [11](11-axiomatic-sequent-systems.html).

## B

**BATNA**. Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement. Section [41](41-negotiation-game-theory.html).

**Bayes' theorem**. $P(A|B) = P(B|A)P(A)/P(B)$. Updates beliefs with evidence. Section [33](33-bayes-theorem.html).

**Biconditional** *(biconcizionale)*. $p \leftrightarrow q$: true when $p, q$ have same truth value. Section [07](07-propositional-logic.html).

**Black swan**. Rare, high-impact, retrospectively explainable event. Taleb. Section [37](37-knightian-black-swans.html).

**Brier score**. Metric of probabilistic forecast quality: $\frac{1}{N}\sum(p_i - o_i)^2$. Section [36](36-forecasting-calibration.html).

## C

**Calibration**. Forecaster is calibrated if "70%" occurs ~70% of times. Section [36](36-forecasting-calibration.html).

**Causality** *(causalità)*. Asymmetric productive relation: $X$ causes $Y$ if intervention on $X$ alters $Y$. Section [45](45-causality-pearl.html).

**CNF**. Conjunctive Normal Form: conjunction of disjunctions of literals. Section [08](08-equivalences-normal-forms.html).

**Cognitive bias**. Systematic judgment distortion. Section [23](23-cognitive-biases.html).

**Common knowledge**. Everyone knows, everyone knows that everyone knows, ad infinitum. Section [17](17-temporal-deontic-epistemic-logic.html).

**Confounder**. Variable causing both $X$ and $Y$, creating spurious correlation. Section [45](45-causality-pearl.html).

**Confirmation bias**. Seeking/remembering information that confirms one's view. Section [23](23-cognitive-biases.html).

**Conjunction fallacy**. Mistake: $P(A \wedge B) > P(A)$. Linda example. Section [23](23-cognitive-biases.html).

**Contradiction** *(contraddizione)*. Always-false formula. Section [07](07-propositional-logic.html).

**Contrapositive**. From $p \rightarrow q$ to $\neg q \rightarrow \neg p$. Logically equivalent. Section [08](08-equivalences-normal-forms.html).

**Counterfactual** *(controfattuale)*. "Had $X$ not occurred, would $Y$?". Third rung of Pearl's ladder. Section [45](45-causality-pearl.html).

**Curry-Howard**. Propositions as types, proofs as programs. Section [19](19-curry-howard-type-theory.html).

## D

**DAG**. Directed Acyclic Graph. Used to model causality. Section [45](45-causality-pearl.html).

**De Morgan's laws**. $\neg(p \wedge q) \equiv \neg p \vee \neg q$ and dual. Section [08](08-equivalences-normal-forms.html).

**Deduction** *(deduzione)*. From general to particular; validity-preserving. Section [03](03-types-of-reasoning.html).

**Natural deduction**. Proof system with I/E rules per connective. Section [10](10-natural-deduction.html).

**Demarcation**. Criterion separating science from non-science. Popper: falsifiability. Section [43](43-scientific-method-popper.html).

**Deontic**. Modal logic for obligations and permissions. Section [17](17-temporal-deontic-epistemic-logic.html).

**Design thinking**. User-centered problem solving methodology: 5 phases. Section [30](30-design-thinking.html).

**Dialectic** *(dialettica)*. Socratic method: question-driven discovery. Section [40](40-debate-dialectic.html).

**Disjunction** *(disgiunzione)*. $p \vee q$. Section [07](07-propositional-logic.html).

**Dual process**. Mind as two systems: S1 (fast), S2 (slow). Section [24](24-dual-process.html).

**Dunning-Kruger**. Overestimation of competence in the least competent. Section [23](23-cognitive-biases.html).

## E

**Endowment effect**. Overvalue what you own. Section [23](23-cognitive-biases.html).

**Epistemology**. Study of knowledge. Section [42](42-epistemology.html).

**Equivocation**. Same term, two meanings, in one argument. Section [22](22-informal-fallacies-presumption.html).

**Heuristic**. Mental shortcut, efficient but sometimes fallacious. Section [26](26-problem-solving-heuristics.html).

**Exercises**. Section [51](51-exercises.html).

## F

**Fallacy**. Argument seeming valid but isn't. Formal or informal. Sections [20](20-formal-fallacies.html)-[22](22-informal-fallacies-presumption.html).

**Falsifiability**. A theory is scientific if confutable by observation in principle. Popper. Section [43](43-scientific-method-popper.html).

**FOL**. First-Order Logic: $\forall, \exists$ over individuals. Section [12](12-predicate-logic-syntax.html), [13](13-predicate-logic-semantics.html).

**Forecasting**. Probabilistic prediction. Section [36](36-forecasting-calibration.html).

**Framing**. Same problem, different descriptions, different choices. Section [35](35-decision-theory.html).

**Frequentism**. Probability as long-run frequency. Section [32](32-probability-foundations.html).

**Fuzzy logic**. Logic with values in $[0,1]$ (Zadeh). Section [18](18-non-classical-logic.html).

## G

**Gettier problem**. JTB doesn't suffice for knowledge (1963). Section [42](42-epistemology.html).

**Gödel's theorems**. First: PA-extending consistent systems have true unprovable sentences. Second: such systems can't prove their own consistency. Section [15](15-metalogic-godel.html).

**Glossary**. This section, [52](52-glossary-az.html).

## H

**Hindsight bias**. Retrospective conviction outcome was predictable. Section [23](23-cognitive-biases.html).

**Hume's problem of induction**. Induction has no rational justification — habit. Section [43](43-scientific-method-popper.html).

## I

**Implication** *(implicazione)*. $p \rightarrow q$. Section [07](07-propositional-logic.html).

**Incommensurability**. Different paradigms share no common language (Kuhn). Section [44](44-kuhn-lakatos-feyerabend.html).

**Induction**. Generalize from observed cases. Section [03](03-types-of-reasoning.html). Also a proof method.

**Inference**. Movement from premises to conclusion. Section [09](09-rules-of-inference.html).

**Intuitionistic logic**. Constructive: rejects excluded middle. Section [18](18-non-classical-logic.html).

## J

**JTB**. Justified True Belief. Classical pre-Gettier theory. Section [42](42-epistemology.html).

## K

**Knightian uncertainty**. Uncertainty with unknown probabilities. Section [37](37-knightian-black-swans.html).

**Kripke semantics**. Modal semantics via possible worlds. Section [16](16-modal-logic.html).

**Kuhn paradigm**. Shared framework within which normal science operates. Section [44](44-kuhn-lakatos-feyerabend.html).

## L

**Lateral thinking**. Non-linear, generative problem solving (de Bono). Section [29](29-creative-thinking.html).

**Likelihood**. $P(\text{evidence} | \text{hypothesis})$. Section [33](33-bayes-theorem.html).

**Logos**. (1) Greek "reason, word". (2) Rational rhetorical appeal. Section [39](39-rhetoric-persuasion.html).

**Loss aversion**. Loss weighs ~2× equivalent gain. Section [35](35-decision-theory.html).

## M

**Mental model**. Conceptual schema for reasoning in a domain. Section [31](31-mental-models.html).

**Metalogic**. Study of logical systems themselves. Section [15](15-metalogic-godel.html).

**Modus ponens**. $p \rightarrow q, p \vdash q$. Section [09](09-rules-of-inference.html).

**Modus tollens**. $p \rightarrow q, \neg q \vdash \neg p$. Section [09](09-rules-of-inference.html).

**Monty Hall**. Probability paradox: always switch. Section [34](34-probability-paradoxes.html).

## N

**Nash equilibrium**. Strategic configuration from which no one improves by unilateral change. Section [41](41-negotiation-game-theory.html).

**Negation** *(negazione)*. $\neg p$. Section [07](07-propositional-logic.html).

## O

**Occam's razor**. Prefer the simplest hypothesis that fits data. Section [31](31-mental-models.html).

**OODA loop**. Observe-Orient-Decide-Act (Boyd). Section [25](25-polya-problem-solving.html).

## P

**Paradox**. Apparently valid argument leading to absurd conclusion. Section [46](46-famous-paradoxes.html).

**Paraconsistent**. Tolerates contradictions without explosion. Section [18](18-non-classical-logic.html).

**Pearl do-calculus**. Formal system for causal inference. Section [45](45-causality-pearl.html).

**Computational thinking**. Decomposition + pattern + abstraction + algorithms. Section [27](27-computational-thinking.html).

**Polya** (4 problem-solving steps). Understand, plan, execute, look back. Section [25](25-polya-problem-solving.html).

**Popper** (falsificationism). Section [43](43-scientific-method-popper.html).

**Predicate** *(predicato)*. Property or relation applied to individuals. Section [12](12-predicate-logic-syntax.html).

**Premise** *(premessa)*. Argument's starting assumption. Section [04](04-anatomy-of-arguments.html).

**Principle of charity**. Interpret others as rationally as possible. Section [40](40-debate-dialectic.html).

**Prior, posterior**. Belief before/after evidence (Bayes). Section [33](33-bayes-theorem.html).

**Problem solving**. Sections [25](25-polya-problem-solving.html)-[28](28-algorithms-strategies.html).

**Proposition** *(proposizione)*. Declarative statement with truth value. Section [07](07-propositional-logic.html).

**Prospect theory**. Descriptive decision theory: reference, loss aversion, framing. Section [35](35-decision-theory.html).

## Q

**Quantifier** *(quantificatore)*. $\forall$ (universal), $\exists$ (existential). Section [12](12-predicate-logic-syntax.html).

## R

**Reasoning** *(ragionamento)*. Drawing conclusions from premises. Section [03](03-types-of-reasoning.html).

**RCT**. Randomized Controlled Trial. Gold standard for causation. Section [45](45-causality-pearl.html).

**Regression to the mean**. Extreme measurements tend toward the average. Section [31](31-mental-models.html).

**Rhetoric** *(retorica)*. Art of persuasion. Ethos, pathos, logos. Section [39](39-rhetoric-persuasion.html).

**Risk vs uncertainty**. Section [37](37-knightian-black-swans.html).

**Russell's paradox**. Set of sets not containing themselves. Section [46](46-famous-paradoxes.html).

## S

**S1, S2**. System 1 and System 2 (Kahneman). Section [24](24-dual-process.html).

**SCAMPER**. Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other use, Eliminate, Reverse. Section [29](29-creative-thinking.html).

**Semantics** *(semantica)*. Study of meaning of formulas. Section [13](13-predicate-logic-semantics.html).

**Sequent** *(sequente)*. $\Gamma \vdash \Delta$. Section [11](11-axiomatic-sequent-systems.html).

**SIFT**. Stop, Investigate, Find, Trace — news literacy. Section [50](50-propaganda-manipulation.html).

**Syllogism** *(sillogismo)*. Aristotelian two-premise argument. Section [02](02-history-of-logic.html).

**Simpson's paradox**. Trend reverses under aggregation. Section [34](34-probability-paradoxes.html).

**Slippery slope**. Fallacy of unjustified causal chain. Section [21](21-informal-fallacies-relevance.html).

**Sorites paradox**. Heap paradox. Section [46](46-famous-paradoxes.html).

**Soundness**. $\vdash \Rightarrow \models$ (derivations preserve truth). Section [15](15-metalogic-godel.html).

**Steelman**. Reconstruct opponent's argument in strongest form. Section [40](40-debate-dialectic.html).

**Straw man**. Fallacy of caricaturing opponent. Section [21](21-informal-fallacies-relevance.html).

**Sunk cost fallacy**. Continuing to "not waste what's spent". Section [23](23-cognitive-biases.html).

**Systems thinking**. Section [47](47-systems-thinking.html).

## T

**Truth table**. Enumeration of T/F assignments. Section [07](07-propositional-logic.html).

**Tautology**. Always-true formula. Section [07](07-propositional-logic.html).

**Game theory**. Formal analysis of strategic interactions. Section [41](41-negotiation-game-theory.html).

**Tit-for-tat**. Winning strategy in repeated games. Section [41](41-negotiation-game-theory.html).

**Toulmin model**. Claim, data, warrant, backing, qualifier, rebuttal. Section [38](38-argumentation-toulmin.html).

**Type theory**. Foundational alternative to set theory via Curry-Howard. Section [19](19-curry-howard-type-theory.html).

## U

**Expected utility**. $\mathbb{E}[u(X)]$. Normative criterion of rational decision. Section [35](35-decision-theory.html).

## V

**Validity** *(validità)*. Argument property: if premises true, conclusion true. Section [04](04-anatomy-of-arguments.html).

**VUCA**. Volatility, Uncertainty, Complexity, Ambiguity. Section [48](48-wicked-problems.html).

**Epistemic virtue**. Cognitive trait for knowledge: open-mindedness, humility. Section [42](42-epistemology.html).

**von Neumann-Morgenstern**. Axiomatic basis of expected utility. Section [35](35-decision-theory.html).

## W

**Warrant**. Implicit justification in a Toulmin argument. Section [38](38-argumentation-toulmin.html).

**Wicked problem**. Problem without shared definition or "right" solution. Section [48](48-wicked-problems.html).

## Z

**Zeno's paradoxes**. Achilles & tortoise, arrow, dichotomy. Section [46](46-famous-paradoxes.html).

**ZOPA**. Zone Of Possible Agreement. Section [41](41-negotiation-game-theory.html).
