Continuity Section 26 of 59 Level: intermediate

Uniform continuity, Heine, Lipschitz

**Uniform continuity** — same $\delta$ for all points, not just 'one $\delta$ per point'. Swapping quantifier order produces a **strictly stronger** notion. Heine-Cantor theorem (compact ⇒ uniform), Lipschitz and Hölder.

Area
Continuity
Prerequisites
  • Continuity (sec. 24)
  • Weierstrass on compacts (sec. 25)
Tools / references
  • Rudin — *Principles*, ch. 4

Quantifier order is everything

Continuity on $A$: $$\forall x_0 \in A,\ \forall \varepsilon > 0,\ \exists \delta > 0 : |x - x_0| < \delta \Rightarrow |f(x) - f(x_0)| < \varepsilon.$$

$\delta$ may depend on both $\varepsilon$ and $x_0$. If we require $\delta$ depend only on $\varepsilon$, we get a strictly stronger notion.

Definition. $f : A \to \mathbb{R}$ is uniformly continuous on $A$ if: $$\forall \varepsilon > 0,\ \exists \delta > 0 : \forall x, y \in A,\ |x - y| < \delta \Rightarrow |f(x) - f(y)| < \varepsilon.$$

Same $\delta$ for every pair. Uniform across the domain.

Negation

$f$ NOT uniformly continuous: find $(x_n), (y_n)$ with $|x_n - y_n| \to 0$ but $|f(x_n) - f(y_n)| \not\to 0$.

Example: $x^2$ on $\mathbb{R}$ — continuous but NOT uniform

$x_n = n$, $y_n = n + 1/n$. $|x_n - y_n| = 1/n \to 0$. But $|y_n^2 - n^2| = 2 + 1/n^2 \ge 2$. With $\varepsilon_0 = 2$, no uniform $\delta$.

Interpretation: at infinity slope of $x^2$ diverges. To maintain same output spread $\varepsilon$, need smaller $\delta$ depending on point.

Heine-Cantor theorem

Theorem. $f$ continuous on a compact $K$ ⇒ $f$ uniformly continuous on $K$.

Practical consequence: on $[a, b]$ closed bounded, continuous ⇒ uniformly continuous.

Proof (by contradiction). Continuous, not uniform: $\exists \varepsilon_0$ and $(x_n), (y_n)$ with $|x_n - y_n| < 1/n$, $|f(x_n) - f(y_n)| \ge \varepsilon_0$. By BW (compact), $x_{n_k} \to x^*$. Also $y_{n_k} \to x^*$. By continuity, $|f(x_{n_k}) - f(y_{n_k})| \to 0$. Contradiction. ∎

Lipschitz functions

Definition. $f$ Lipschitz with constant $L$ if $$|f(x) - f(y)| \le L |x - y|.$$

Lipschitz ⇒ uniformly continuous ($\delta = \varepsilon/L$). Strictly stronger.

Examples: - $\sin x, \cos x$ Lipschitz with $L = 1$. - $|x|$ Lipschitz with $L = 1$. - $\sqrt x$ on $[0, 1]$ NOT Lipschitz (slope blows up at 0), but is uniformly continuous.

Hölder functions

Definition. $f$ Hölder with exponent $\alpha \in (0, 1]$ if $$|f(x) - f(y)| \le C |x - y|^\alpha.$$

$\alpha = 1$ is Lipschitz. For $\alpha < 1$, "weaker".

Hierarchy: Lipschitz $\subset$ Hölder $\subset$ uniform $\subset$ continuous.

Examples

Uniformly continuous: - Continuous on compacts (Heine-Cantor). - $\sin x$ on $\mathbb{R}$ (Lipschitz). - $\sqrt x$ on $[0, +\infty)$.

NOT uniformly continuous: - $x^2$ on $\mathbb{R}$. - $1/x$ on $(0, 1]$. - $\sin(1/x)$ on $(0, 1]$.

Exercises

Exercise 1 — Lipschitz Show $\sin x$ Lipschitz with $L = 1$. **Solution.** $|\sin x - \sin y| = 2 |\sin((x-y)/2)| |\cos((x+y)/2)| \le 2 \cdot |x-y|/2 = |x - y|$. ∎
Exercise 2 — Not uniform on open interval $1/x$ on $(0, 1]$. **Solution.** $x_n = 1/n$, $y_n = 1/(n+1)$. $|x_n - y_n| \to 0$, $|f(x_n) - f(y_n)| = 1$. Not uniform. ∎
Exercise 3 — Heine-Cantor Show $\sin(x^2)$ uniformly continuous on $[0, 10]$. **Solution.** $[0, 10]$ compact, $\sin(x^2)$ continuous. Heine-Cantor. ∎

One-line takeaway

Uniform continuity = same $\delta$ for all points (not point-by-point) — strictly stronger than continuity, but coincident on compacts (Heine-Cantor).
